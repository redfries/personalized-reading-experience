I am working on AI tools that personalize the experience of reading academic papers for real human use, not for show. My focus is on helping students and researchers cut through dense text, stay focused on what matters, and actually remember what they read. I am especially interested in human computer interaction, reading efficiency, attention guidance, and how highlights or cues inside the paper can steer the reader toward the most relevant ideas instead of wasting time scanning everything line by line.

Technically, I care about systems that use sentence level embeddings, user profiles, and semantic similarity to decide which sentences deserve attention. I look for work on personalized reading interfaces, adaptive highlights, intelligent tutoring like behaviors, and serious evaluation of tools that support academic reading and retention. My current scope does not include pure hardware work or unrelated domains such as EV batteries or sports analytics, because the energy of this project is aimed at one thing only: making academic reading sharper, faster, and more aligned with the reader’s own research goals.
